Mary Jackson

A pioneer in science and engineering. Her work is one of the reasons why NASA could put people into space.

Mary Jackson in 1977, doing research at the Langley Centre.

Mary's Accomplishments

  • Paved the way for black people and women in the field of science and engineering.
  • First African American female engineer at NASA.
  • Originally she worked at NASA as a computor, doing mathematical calculations for NASA manually, before the technology of computors replaced this type of work.
  • Lived from April 9, 1921 to February 11, 2005.
