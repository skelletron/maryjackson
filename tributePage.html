Mary Jackson

A pioneer in science and engineering. Her contribution was integral to NASA's space program.

Mary Jackson in 1977, doing research at the Langley Centre.

Some of her Story

  • Paved the way for black people and women in the field of science and engineering.
  • First African American female engineer at NASA.
  • Originally she worked at NASA as a human computor, doing mathematical calculations for their space program manually, before the technology of computors replaced this type of work.
  • In honour of her legacy, NASA's agency headquarters building in Washington, D.C was renamed the Mary W. Jackson NASA Headquarters.
  • Lived from April 9, 1921 to February 11, 2005.
